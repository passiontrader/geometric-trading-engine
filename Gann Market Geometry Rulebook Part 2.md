## MODULE 11: SQUARE OF 9 AND SQUARE OF 4

### Definition: Time/Price Spiral Charts

**RULE:** The Square of 9 (odd contracts) and Square of 4 (even contracts) are the primary tools for identifying price and time resistance at natural number completions. Determine which to use by counting total contract days: odd days → Square of 9; even days → Square of 4.

---

**RULE 130-SQUARE OF 9 CIRCLE ENDS:** Resistance at end of each circle: 9, 25, 49, 81, 121, 169, 225, 289, 361. These are the squares of odd numbers. Each circle completion = natural resistance in both price and time.

**RULE 131-SQUARE OF 4 CIRCLE ENDS:** Resistance at end of each circle: 4, 16, 36, 64, 100, 144, 196, 256, 324, 400. These are the squares of even numbers.

**RULE 132-FIXED CROSS (VERTICAL/HORIZONTAL):** Numbers on the vertical and horizontal lines through the center = primary resistance. Square of 9 vertical (up): 4, 15, 34, 61, 96, 129, 190, 249, 316. Vertical (down): 8, 23, 46, 77, 116, 163, 218, 281, 352. Horizontal (right): 6, 19, 40, 69, 106, 151, 204, 265, 334. Horizontal (left): 2, 11, 28, 53, 86, 127, 176, 233, 298.

**RULE 133-CARDINAL SQUARE (DIAGONAL):** Numbers on X diagonals from center = secondary resistance. Square of 9: 3, 13, 31, 57, 91, 133, 183, 241, 307 and 7, 21, 43, 73, 111, 157, 211, 273, 343.

**RULE 134-PRICE SUPPORT/RESISTANCE FROM CENTER:** Set center = contract low. Moving out 45°, 90°, 120°, 180° gives resistance prices above. Moving to adjacent degrees gives support prices below.
- From price 496: 45° below = 485, 90° = 474, 120° = 463, 180° = 452
- From price 496: 45° above = 507, 90° = 518, 120° = 529, 180° = 541

**RULE 135-TIME RESISTANCE FROM CENTER:** Set center = start date of contract or major low date. Moving 45, 90, 120, 144, 180, 216, 270, 315, 360 calendar days from that date = probable trend change dates.
- Example: Start date March 21 → 45° = May 6; 90° = June 21; 120° = Aug 8; 180° = Sep 23

**RULE 136-PRICE ASPECTING:** When the date on the outside of the wheel hits the same angle position as the price inside the wheel simultaneously → time is aspecting price. Natural resistance. Very high probability reversal point.

**RULE 137-MAIN TREND ANGLE TEST:** If counter-trend rallies stop EXACTLY on 90° angle lines → main trend is DOWN. If counter-trend declines stop EXACTLY on 90° angle lines → main trend is UP. If the main thrust of trend ALSO stops on an angle line → trend is reversing.

**RULE 138-TREND IMPORTANCE RANKING (90° cycles from center):**
1. 90 day (rank 1 — highest)
2. 45 day (rank 2)
3. 180 day (rank 3)
4. 135 day (rank 4)
5. 270 day (rank 5)
6. 120 day (rank 6)
7. 315 day (rank 7)
8. 225 day (rank 8)

**RULE 139-MAJOR YEAR CHANGE:** Major yearly trends terminate on their anniversary dates. Confirmed by minor trend reversals (45-day, 90-day) occurring at the same time.

### Rules of Vibration of Trend Direction

**RULE 140-VIBRATION RULE 1:** When vibrations of trend start moving from LOW to HIGH → trend is TURNING DOWN.

**RULE 141-VIBRATION RULE 2:** When vibrations of trend start moving from HIGH to LOWER HIGH → trend is TURNING DOWN.

**RULE 142-VIBRATION RULE 3:** When vibrations of trend start moving from HIGH to LOW → trend is TURNING UP.

**RULE 143-VIBRATION RULE 4:** When vibrations of trend start moving from LOW to HIGHER LOW → trend is TURNING UP.

### Hexagon and Octagon Charts

**RULE 144-HEXAGON:** Each successive circle of hexagon increases by 6 more than prior increase (6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36, 42, 48, 54, 60, 66...). Circles end at 7, 19, 37, 61, 91, 127, 169, 217, 271, 331, 397. Shows why markets move faster at higher levels.

**RULE 145-OCTAGON:** Each successive circle of octagon increases by 8 more than prior. Circles end at 25, 49, 81, 121, 169... Markets widen range between angles at each successive square outward.

### Circle Charts (12, 18, 48)

**RULE 146-CIRCLE CHARTS:** Set center to 1 (or starting price). Important highs/lows of a market land on the same diagonal radiating from center. Circle chart of 12 uses multiplier 24; Circle of 18 uses multiplier 36; Circle of 48 uses multiplier 50.

**RULE 147-CIRCLE DIAGONAL IDENTIFICATION:** Circle back and identify which diagonal your market's highs/lows consistently land on. When market inverts (high instead of low on same diagonal) → still respects diagonal timing.

## MODULE 12: FORECASTING TIME

### Definition: Time Forecasting

**RULE:** The future is a repetition of the past. All major time cycles must be identified, compared to current market, and used in combination for timing precision. When 3+ timing projections cluster within 3 days → high probability reversal point.

---

### Master Cycles (Long-Term)

**RULE 148-120 YEAR CYCLE:** = 6× 20-year, 4× 30-year, 2× 60-year. Compare current market to 120 years ago for pattern matching.

**RULE 149-90 YEAR CYCLE:** = 3× 30-year, 1.5× 60-year. Very important.

**RULE 150-60 YEAR MASTER CYCLE:** Primary master cycle. Find past 60-year cycles; compare to current. Divide by 10 for 6-year harmonics.

**RULE 151-49/50 YEAR CYCLE:** Contains seven 7-year sub-cycles. Last 7-year cycle in 49-year sequence is usually down.

**RULE 152-30 YEAR CYCLE:** ½ of 60-year master.

**RULE 153-10 YEAR CYCLE:** 6 per 60-year cycle; 5 per 50-year cycle; 3 per 30-year cycle.

**RULE 154-7 YEAR CYCLE:** 7 per 49-year cycle; 14 per 98-year cycle.

**RULE 155-5 YEAR CYCLE:** Smallest cycle for historical comparison purposes.

**RULE 156-2.5–3.5 YEAR CYCLE:** Most counter-trends react with this cycle duration.

**RULE 157-CYCLE HARMONICS:** Divide any major cycle by 10 to get harmonic intervals. When 60-year and 90-year cycles both point same direction → near certainty of market moving that direction.

### Using Cycles Operationally

**RULE 158-OVERLAY SAME HARMONIC YEARS:** Print charts of each harmonic year on translucent paper. Overlay on current year. When majority of harmonic cycles show same direction → high confidence in that direction.

**RULE 159-CYCLE SHIFT:** Anniversary dates may shift several days from exact date due to time progression. Slide harmonic year chart left or right vs. current chart to align swings. Note the offset.

**RULE 160-KEY TIME PERIOD BEGINNINGS:** Watch first 5 days of:
- January 21 (yearly)
- March 21 (Spring Equinox)
- June 21 (Summer Solstice / semi-annual)
- September 21 (Fall Equinox)
- April / October (quarterly)
Trend started during these periods typically runs 3–4 months.

**RULE 161-YEAR DIVISIONS TO WATCH:**
- ÷2 = 6 months
- ÷4 = 3 months (quarters)
- ÷8 = 1.5 months (22.5 days)
- ÷16 = ~22.5 days
- ÷32 = 11.25 days
Watch first 5 days of each division for trend change.

**RULE 162-SAME DIGIT YEAR COMPARISON:** Overlay all years ending in same digit as current year (e.g., 1995: compare 1985, 1975, 1965...). Markets that had highs in years ending with same digit may repeat highs.

### Using Beginning Numbers for Forecasting

**RULE 163-BEGINNING NUMBER RIPPLE:** When market makes major bottom at price X, it will move up X minutes, X hours, X days, X weeks, X months, X years from that bottom.

**RULE 164-TOP NUMBER RIPPLE:** When market makes major top at price Y, it will move down Y minutes, Y hours, Y days, Y weeks, Y months, Y years from that top.

**RULE 165-HARMONIC PROJECTIONS FROM BEGINNING NUMBER:** For a bottom at 43: project forward 43, 86, 129, 172... days/weeks/months. Each interval = potential turning point.

**RULE 166-CIRCLE NUMBERS FROM TOPS/BOTTOMS:** From any important top or bottom, project forward: 45, 72, 90, 120, 144, 216, 240, 270, 360 time units. When 3+ cluster on same date → major timing point.

### Wave-Based Timing

**RULE 167-FIBONACCI TIME RATIOS:** Use these ratios between past tops/bottoms to project future turning points: 0.382, 0.500, 0.618, 1.000, 1.382, 1.500, 1.618, 2.000, 2.382, 2.500, 2.618.

**RULE 168-TIMING CLUSTER:** When 3+ Fibonacci time projections from different prior wave pairs fall within 3 days of each other → significant pivot. When major, intermediate, AND minor degree all cluster → extremely important pivot.

**RULE 169-ALL DEGREES MUST AGREE:** For valid timing signal: minor (daily), intermediate (weekly), AND major (monthly) time cycles must all point to same timeframe without conflict.

**RULE 170-REOCCURRING CYCLE COUNTS:** Track and record: High-to-Low, Low-to-High, Top-to-Top, Bottom-to-Bottom intervals in both calendar days and market days. What the market used before it will use again.

### Intraday Timing

**RULE 171-INTRADAY FIBONACCI:** Fibonacci ratios applied to intraday swings can project turns within 5 minutes on 1-minute charts. When intraday count = Gann circle number (45, 90, 120 hours etc.) → higher probability.

## MODULE 13: FORECASTING PRICE

### Definition: Price Forecasting

**RULE:** Price must ultimately square with time. Use multiple methods to arrive at price clusters — the more methods that agree on the same price level, the more significant that level.

---

### Table Chart Price Method

**RULE 172-TABLE CHART PRICE USE:** Set table chart base (number 1) to contract low or opening price. Top/bottom/midpoint numbers of each row = probable price highs and lows. When market price is at a top row → expect resistance; bottom row → expect support.

**RULE 173-NATURAL TABLE PRICE:** Leave table chart base at 1. Natural top, bottom, and midpoint numbers of the table = permanent resistance/support for all markets. NYSE: resistance every 20 price points (square of 20).

### Circle and Spiral Chart Price Method

**RULE 174-CIRCLE CHART PRICE:** Set center to contract low, high, or starting price. Numbers on the same radiating line from center = future support/resistance prices. When price hits the end of each circle → strong resistance.

**RULE 175-HEXAGON PRICE:** Set center to starting price. Resistance at each successive hexagon ring outward. Used to identify why prices move faster at higher levels — distance between rings expands.

### Square of 9/4 for Price

**RULE 176-SQ9 BEGINNING PRICE:** Set center of Square of 9 to contract low. Resistance at end of each circle outward (squares of odd numbers). Prior resistance becomes support once exceeded.

**RULE 177-SQ9 NATURAL NUMBERS:** Leave center at 1. Fixed cross and cardinal square numbers are natural resistance for all markets.

**RULE 178-SQ9 GANN WHEEL — PRICE USE:** Set overlay 0 point to price of major low. Count 90° → resistance price. Count 180° → stronger resistance. 270°, 360° further out. If counter-trend stops EXACTLY on a 90° line → confirms main trend direction. If main thrust also stops on 90° line → main trend reversing.

**RULE 179-EXPANDING WAVE LIMITATION:** Square of 9 and 4 designed for expanding waves (rising prices). For contracting (falling) markets, use multiple price projection spreadsheet instead.

### ABC Fibonacci Price Projection

**RULE 180-ABC RATIO METHOD:** From any A-B-C wave structure:
- Projection = A to B distance × ratio + C
- Ratios: 0.382, 0.618, 1.000, 1.382, 1.500, 1.618, 2.000, 2.382, 2.500, 2.618

**RULE 181-A1 TO A2 RETRACEMENTS:** Market retraces 0.382, 0.500, or 0.618 of prior advance. Average multiple A1-A2 retracements to find the dominant retracement ratio for that specific market.

**RULE 182-ELLIOTT PRICE FORMULAS:**
- c = (a − b) + c
- d = c − (c − b)
- g = d + (a − 0) × 2.618
- i = d + (c − d) × 4.236
- m = l + 1.618 × (a − 0)
If actual price at wave terminal point does not match formula output → wave count is wrong; re-label.

### Master Overlay Price Method

**RULE 183-OVERLAY VARIABLE PRICE:** From every major top or bottom, price will advance or decline 45, 52, 72, 90, 120, 144, 216, 240, 270, 360 price points. Also use multiples of overlay squares (3 squares of 144 = 432 point move from bottom).

**RULE 184-OVERLAY FIXED PRICE:** From price 0 base: fixed resistance at 45, 90, 144, 288, 432, 576, 720, 864, 1008... Combine variable and fixed overlays for cluster confirmation.

**RULE 185-MARKET SQUARE IDENTIFICATION:** Each market works primarily in one natural square: 45, 52, 90, 120, 144, 216, 240, 270, or 360. On daily charts, most common: square of 90 or 144. Experiment to identify the correct square for each market.

**RULE 186-ALL-TIME-LOW SQUARE:** May Soybeans all-time low = 67 → use square of 67 as the market's permanent overlay. Combine with fixed natural squares.

**RULE 187-CLUSTER CONFIRMATION:** The more price methods that agree on the same level, the more important that price level. Minimum 3 methods confirming same price = trade-worthy level.

## MODULE 14: SWING CHARTS

### Definition: Swing Charts

**RULE:** Swing charts define trend direction and provide objective entry, stop, and trend-change signals across all timeframes. The 3-day swing defines main trend; 1-day swing defines entry timing.

---

**RULE 188-1-DAY SWING:** In rising market, move trend line up until prior day's low is broken. When broken, move trend line down until prior day's high is broken. Defines short-term trend.

**RULE 189-3-DAY SWING:** In rising market, keep trend line up until low of last 3 days is broken. When broken, move down until high of last 3 days is broken. Defines main trend.

**RULE 190-COMBINED SWING SYSTEM:** When 3-day swing is long → use 1-day swing to enter (buy when 1-day swing breaks up after pullback). When 3-day swing is short → use 1-day swing to enter short.

**RULE 191-PRICE-BASED SWING:** Swing can also be defined by price instead of days: in corn, when prior day's low is broken by 2+ cents → trend turns down (regardless of day count).

**RULE 192-TIME AND PRICE COMBINED SWING:** For confirmation, require both time criterion (3-day rule) AND price criterion (2-cent break in corn) to be satisfied simultaneously before trend changes.

**RULE 193-STOP PLACEMENT — SWING:** Place stops below last important swing low (long positions) or above last important swing high (short positions).

**RULE 194-OVERBALANCE TRIGGER:** Do not call main trend reversed until current correction exceeds the largest prior correction in both time AND price. Example: if prior corrections were max 10 days and 20 cents, trend has NOT reversed until current correction exceeds 10 days AND 20 cents.

**RULE 195-PERCENTAGE RETRACEMENT:** In bull market, percentage retracements should be getting smaller as market advances. When retracement percentage increases above prior retracement → momentum declining → possible top.

**RULE 196-SWING PERCENTAGE DECLINE:** When percentage of last two price swings in direction of trend is declining → possible trend change approaching.

**RULE 197-TIME IS SUPERIOR TO PRICE:** Percentage retracement (time-based) more reliable than fixed-price retracement for detecting trend changes.

**RULE 198-REVERSAL DAY CONFIRMATION:** When swing chart is broken AND a reversal day occurs simultaneously → higher confidence trend change signal. Three reversal types: (1) gap up and reverse, (2) hook reversal, (3) key reversal.

## MODULE 15: GAPS

### Definition: Gap Types

**RULE:** Gaps reveal where the market is in its Elliott wave structure. Gap type determines whether a gap marks beginning, midpoint, or end of a move.

---

**RULE 199-ORDINARY GAPS:** Occur within normal trading range. No special significance. Usually filled quickly. Do not trade on ordinary gaps alone.

**RULE 200-BREAKAWAY GAP:** Occurs when price breaks out of a sideways trading range. NEVER filled (if filled → move fails). Accompanied by big volume. Wave context: occurs at end of corrective waves (2, 4) launching impulse moves. Go with the breakout direction.

**RULE 201-MEASURING GAP:** Occurs mid-move in an accelerated trend. High volume. Reactions against main trend almost nil. Identifies market's midpoint — project equal distance from measuring gap to estimate target.
- Formula: Measuring gap price + (measuring gap price − move start) = approximate target
- Wave context: wave 3 and wave 5 fast moves

**RULE 202-EXHAUSTION GAP:** Occurs in last stage of fast-moving market. Result of final panic buying/selling. After exhaustion gap: watch for reversal day within a few days → confirms major high/low.
- Wave context: wave 5 and final C waves
- SKIP trading in direction of exhaustion gap

**RULE 203-GAP FILL FAILURE:** If a breakaway gap is subsequently filled → breakout has failed. Reverse position or exit.

## MODULE 16: TOPS AND BOTTOMS

### Definition: Double/Triple Tops and Bottoms

**RULE:** Double and triple tops/bottoms are primary trading setups. Sell double/triple tops with stop above; buy double/triple bottoms with stop below. The larger the timeframe, the more significant the setup.

---

**RULE 204-TIMEFRAME IMPORTANCE:** Monthly double/triple top > Weekly double/triple top > Daily double/triple top in significance and expected move size.

**RULE 205-BOTTOMS MORE COMMON THAN TOPS:** Double/triple bottoms form more frequently and are easier to identify. Bottoms form on low volume; tops form on high volume.

**RULE 206-RISING BOTTOMS STRONGER:** In a double/triple bottom: rising bottoms (each successive bottom higher than prior) = stronger than flat bottoms. Market is not reaching prior weakness.

**RULE 207-DISPLACED MA METHOD:** Define double/triple tops/bottoms using 2-day moving average displaced 2 days to the right.
1. Market must close under the displaced MA before forming a top
2. Multiple tops (1, 3, 5) with each approaching then closing under MA
3. When market closes below the prior swing low (point 2) → confirmed breakdown
4. Back-test this MA period and displacement for each specific market

**RULE 208-FOURTH ATTEMPT RULE:** When market makes a 4th attempt at prior top/bottom and fails → expect fast move in opposite direction. When market breaks the displaced MA 3 times and then reverses through the 4th top → reverse and go with market direction.

**RULE 209-TRIPLE BOTTOM WAVE CONTEXT:** A triple bottom at beginning of a major 5-wave impulse move = highest probability setup. Must know Elliott wave position to validate.

**RULE 210-BREAKOUT RETEST RULE:** After breakout from double/triple bottom: any pullback (reaction) must NOT exceed 3 weeks. If 4th week shows lower high AND falls below 3rd week's low → uptrend is probably over.

**RULE 211-FOURTH AND FIFTH ATTEMPT SEQUENCE:**
- 3 tops → breakdown → 4th and 5th attempts at lower lows → very weak → expect sharp breakdown
- 3 bottoms → breakup → 4th and 5th bottoms at higher lows → very strong → expect sharp advance

## MODULE 17: VOLUME AND OPEN INTEREST

### Definition: Volume and Open Interest

**RULE:** Volume confirms price trend. Open interest indicates position accumulation or distribution. All three (price, volume, open interest) must be used together for full confirmation.

---

**RULE 212-BREAKOUT VOLUME CONFIRMATION:** Price breaking out of consolidation WITH increasing volume → valid breakout; expect continuation. Price breaking out WITHOUT volume increase → likely false breakout; price will return to consolidation.

**RULE 213-DECLINE VOLUME:** Price falling with volume falling → market may be ready to reverse UP. Price falling to base with volume RISING → accumulation (traders buying as bargain).

**RULE 214-RALLY TO RESISTANCE VOLUME:** Volume increases as price rallies to resistance → distribution (traders unloading at resistance). Price unlikely to break through immediately.

**RULE 215-VOLUME AT STOPS:** Volume spike when market runs into stop cluster areas (above highs/below lows) = floor traders running stops. If market does not continue after stop-run → price will revert. Weak shorts have been squeezed.

**RULE 216-TREND CONTINUATION VOLUME:** For a big trend to continue, volume must continue rising. Declining volume during trend advance → trend losing momentum.

**RULE 217-BLOW-OFF VOLUME:** After a long advance: dramatic volume increase by small speculators near top. After a long decline: dramatic volume increase as losing longs capitulate. Both signal imminent reversal.

**RULE 218-FIRST ADVANCE VOLUME PATTERN (BULL):** Price breaks UP out of consolidation → makes first advance → declines. If volume rises then declines on that first advance/retreat → bullish.

**RULE 219-FIRST DECLINE VOLUME PATTERN (BEAR):** Price breaks DOWN out of consolidation → makes first decline on heavy volume → retraces on declining volume → bearish continuation signal.

**RULE 220-OI RISING + PRICE RISING:** Rising prices + rising volume + rising open interest = all three rising → very bullish confirmation of trend continuation.

**RULE 221-OI FALLING + PRICE RISING:** Rising prices + rising volume + FALLING open interest → short covering, not new buying. Trend will not extend much further.

**RULE 222-OI RISING + PRICE STABLE:** Prices stable + rising open interest = position accumulation. Especially significant at wave 2 or wave 4 support levels.

**RULE 223-OI FALLING + PRICE STABLE:** Prices stable + falling open interest = market losing interest. Public moving elsewhere. Breakout will be needed to change condition.

## MODULE 18: GANN CHANNELS

### Definition: Price Channels

**RULE:** Gann channels bound price movement between parallel geometric angles. The channel height is determined by the specific overlay square the market is working in. Price stays within channel height until the market accelerates or reverses.

---

**RULE 224-CHANNEL ANGLE SET:** Draw angles from 0-point (zero square below wave start) AND from 1 square up from zero. Standard angle set: 1×8, 1×4, 1×3, 1×2, 3×4, 2/3, 1×1, 4×3, 3×2, 2×1, 3×1, 4×1, 8×1.

**RULE 225-CHANNEL HEIGHT IDENTIFICATION:** Channel height = one overlay square in the market's working square.
- Square of 144: units = 144÷8 = 18, multiples: 2.25, 4.5, 9, 18, 36, 72
- Square of 120: units = 120÷8 = 15, multiples: 7.5, 15, 30, 60
- Square of 90: units = 90÷8 = 11.25, multiples: 5.625, 11.25, 22.50, 45, 90
- Square of 52: units = 52÷8 = 6.5, multiples: 13, 26, 46

**RULE 226-CHANNEL ACCELERATION:** If volume increases significantly, channel may expand to next multiple in the overlay square.

**RULE 227-CHANNEL TOP/BOTTOM TRADE:** When price reaches top of channel AND stochastic shows divergence → take profits on long, consider short. When price reaches bottom of channel AND stochastic shows divergence AND MACD above center → buy.

**RULE 228-BUY CHANNEL BOTTOM (WAVE 2 OR 4):** At wave 2(C) or wave 4(C) bottoms: MACD must be above center line (strong position) AND stochastic must be at 20% or below with double bottom and divergence. Buy when market makes new daily high after stochastic turns up.

**RULE 229-SHORT AFTER WAVE 5 TOP:** After wave 5 top, when MACD crosses below center line → sell B-wave (first ABC correction bounce). Use reverse of long entry rules.

**RULE 230-CHANNEL SQUARE:** Draw a square from the start of an angle equal in height and width to the channel measurement. Use the top/bottom of this square for time and price projection targets.

## MODULE 19: TYPES OF ORDERS

**RULE 231-MARKET ORDER USE:** Use market order only when immediate execution outweighs price precision. Expect 1–3 point slippage to floor scalpers.

**RULE 232-OR BETTER ORDER USE:** Place above market for buys (or lower for sells) at exact calculated price target. If not filled within specified time after timing point → change to market order.

**RULE 233-MIT ORDER USE:** Market-if-touched: placed at calculated price target. When price touches the level → order filled at market (may execute at less favorable price). Use when you have high confidence in an exact price target and want automatic execution.

**RULE 234-MARKET-ON-CLOSE USE:** Used by day traders who must be flat at day end. Can also be limit: price must not exceed limit or order does not fill.

**RULE 235-STOPS — MANDATORY:** Always place protective stop immediately upon entering position. Stops placed at: (1) swing low/high using time-price-percent swing chart method, or (2) 10% of excess margin capital limit.

**RULE 236-TIME STOP:** If position shows no profit within 3 days → exit position. Exchanges do not accept time stops; trader must execute manually. Prevents capital sitting in stagnant trade.

**RULE 237-STOP PRESERVATION:** Using proper swing chart stops allows staying in market for the entire move. Close stops from small capital lead to premature stops.

## MODULE 20: COMPLETE TRADING PLAN

### Definition: Trading Plan Execution

**RULE:** Every trade must be part of a complete campaign plan from beginning to end. Trade 3(5) → exit 2/3 at first target → trail stop on remainder to breakeven → re-enter at wave 4 corrective bottom.

---

**RULE 238-HARMONIC YEAR SCAN:** First: scan long-term charts for harmonic year comparisons (5, 10, 15, 30, 45, 60, 90, 100 years back). Confirm current year's pattern matches harmonic template before proceeding.

**RULE 239-CYCLE ANALYSIS:** Next: apply all Gann cycle techniques (top-to-top, bottom-to-bottom, top-to-bottom counts). Identify where next major low or high should be based on pattern AND anniversary dates.

**RULE 240-MULTI-CHART CONFIRMATION:** All four chart types must confirm overbalance of time, price, and percentage before entry: quarterly, monthly, weekly, daily.

**RULE 241-MASTER OVERLAYS FOR CYCLE:** Use master overlays (90, 52, 144 squares) to pinpoint next cycle low. Cross-reference with Fibonacci and circle number projections.

**RULE 242-VIBRATION AND SQ9/4 CHECK:** Check market's vibration rate using square table charts. Use Square of 9 or 4 for time counts from contract beginning.

### Entry Sequence (5-Wave Bull Campaign)

**RULE 243-WAIT FOR PATTERN:** After confirming major bottom at point 0, wait for: wave 1 up, wave (A) down, wave (B) up, wave 2(C) bottom to form. Do NOT enter at point 0.

**RULE 244-ENTRY AT WAVE 2(C):** Buy 3 contracts at wave 2(C). Place stop below point 0.

**RULE 245-SCALE OUT AT WAVE (1) OF 3:** When market advances to first sub-wave peak (1) of wave 3 → sell 2 of 3 contracts. Move stop on remaining 1 contract to breakeven (including commissions).

**RULE 246-RE-ENTER AT WAVE 4(C):** When main wave 3(5) is reached, wait for wave (A), (B), 4(C) correction to complete. Wait for sub-wave 1, (a), (b), 2(c) of the 5th wave to form. Buy 3 contracts at 2(c) with stop below 4(C) bottom.

**RULE 247-SCALE OUT AT WAVE 5 TOP:** When wave 5 top is reached → sell all remaining positions. Complete the campaign.

**RULE 248-BREAKEVEN STOP:** After selling initial 2/3 of position at first target, trailing stop on remaining position MUST be at breakeven or better at all times. No risk remaining.

### Pre-Trade Validation

**RULE 249-DOUBLE AND TRIPLE TOP REACTION RULE:** After breakout from double/triple bottom, reactions must not exceed 3 weeks. If 4th week has lower high and breaks below 3rd week's low → uptrend is over.

**RULE 250-VOLUME MUST CONFIRM CYCLES:** Cycle timing point alone is insufficient. Volume must expand in the direction of expected move to confirm the cycle is active.

**RULE 251-DAILY CHART RULES CHECK:** Every technique learned must be proven on historical data for the specific market being traded. What worked in the past for that market will work again.

- ✗ Stop size would exceed 10% of excess margin capital
